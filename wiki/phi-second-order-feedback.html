Second-Order Phi Feedback Equation (Kp-Only)

Eliminating \(T_2\) from the dual-KID model to expose the damped-oscillator structure for proportional heater feedback (\(K_d=0\)).

Starting Point

This page uses the row-2 and row-4 equations from the current dual-KID model in python-simple-abs/sensor.py. The model is linearized in frequency with only constant and \(i\omega\) terms retained, so \(i\omega \leftrightarrow d/dt\) in the time-domain equations.

The state vector is

\[ y(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} r \\ \phi \\ T_1 \\ T_2 \end{bmatrix}, \]

where

  • row 1 is the resonator amplitude variable \(r\),
  • row 2 is the resonator phase variable \(\phi\) (the \(Q\)-like channel used for feedback),
  • row 3 is the KID1/island1 temperature variable \(T_1\),
  • row 4 is the KID2/island2 temperature variable \(T_2\).

The full frequency-domain linear system is

\[ M(\omega)\,y(\omega)=0 \]

for the deterministic feedback analysis on this page, with

\[ M(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} m_{11} & m_{12} & m_{13} & m_{14} \\ m_{21} & m_{22} & m_{23} & m_{24} \\ m_{31} & m_{32} & m_{33} & 0 \\ m_{41} & m_{42} & 0 & m_{44} \end{bmatrix}. \]

The full model allows both proportional and derivative heater feedback in \(m_{42}\):

\[ m_{42}=Q_r x(\beta_A+2)P_2-K_p-i\omega K_d. \]

This page is the proportional-only specialization \(K_d=0\), so the entries used here are

\[ m_{11}=\frac{2 i \omega Q_i}{\omega_0}+\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+\beta_A+4Q_iQ_r x^2 \]

\[ m_{12}=-\frac{4 i \omega Q_i Q_r x}{\omega_0} \]

\[ m_{13}=-(2Q_r x\,\beta_A)+\frac{2\alpha_A}{T_0} \]

\[ m_{14}=\frac{2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}} \]

\[ m_{21}=+\frac{4 i \omega Q_i Q_r x}{\omega_0}-\beta_\phi \]

\[ m_{22}=\frac{2 i \omega Q_i}{\omega_0}+\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+4Q_iQ_r x^2+2Q_r x\,\beta_\phi \]

\[ m_{23}=-\frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0} \]

\[ m_{24}=-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}} \]

\[ m_{31}=-\left(1+\frac{\beta_A}{2}\right)P_1 \]

\[ m_{32}=Q_r x(\beta_A+2)P_1 \]

\[ m_{33}=i\omega C_1+G_1-\frac{P_1\alpha_A}{T_0} \]

\[ m_{41}=-\left(1+\frac{\beta_A}{2}\right)P_2 \]

\[ m_{42}=Q_r x(\beta_A+2)P_2-K_p \]

\[ m_{44}=i\omega C_2+G_2-\frac{P_2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}}. \]

The row-2 and row-4 equations used below are obtained by taking just the \(\phi\) and \(T_2\) rows of this full matrix equation. Row 3, through \(T_1\), is retained later only as an external forcing term in the reduced scalar equation.

Definitions:

\[ \omega_0 = 2\pi f_0,\qquad x = \frac{\Delta f}{f_0},\qquad Q_r^{-1} = Q_i^{-1} + Q_c^{-1}. \]

The KID2 operating temperature in the matrix is denoted \(T_{02}\) below. In code this is the effective derived temperature of island 2 after resonator dissipation and heater offset are included.

Coupled \(\phi\)-\(T_2\) Equations

The row-2 phase equation is

\[ \frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\,\dot\phi + \frac{4Q_iQ_r x}{\omega_0}\,\dot r - \beta_\phi r + \left(\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+4Q_iQ_r x^2+2Q_r x\,\beta_\phi\right)\phi - \frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}T_1 - \frac{2\alpha_{\phi 2}}{T_{02}}T_2 = 0. \]

The row-4 thermal equation for island 2 is

\[ C_2 \dot T_2 + \left(G_2-\frac{P_2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}}\right)T_2 + \left(Q_r x(\beta_A+2)P_2-K_p\right)\phi - \left(1+\frac{\beta_A}{2}\right)P_2 r = 0. \]

These are the only two equations needed for the scalar second-order \(\phi\) equation. The variables \(r\) and \(T_1\) are kept as source terms on the right-hand side after elimination. Noise drives are omitted on this page because the focus here is the deterministic feedback dynamics.

Eliminated Second-Order Equation

Eliminating \(T_2\) gives a scalar second-order ODE for \(\phi\):

\[ M_\phi\,\ddot\phi+\Gamma_\phi\,\dot\phi+K_\phi\,\phi=S_\phi(t) \]

with coefficients

\[ M_\phi = C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0} \]

\[ \Gamma_\phi = C_2\left(\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+4Q_iQ_r x^2+2Q_r x\,\beta_\phi\right) + \left(G_2-\frac{P_2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}}\right)\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0} \]

\[ K_\phi = \left(G_2-\frac{P_2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}}\right) \left(\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+4Q_iQ_r x^2+2Q_r x\,\beta_\phi\right) + \frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}} \left(Q_r x(\beta_A+2)P_2-K_p\right). \]

The deterministic source term is

\[ S_\phi(t) = S_r(t) + S_{T1}(t). \]

Explicit Forcing Terms

The terms involving the readout amplitude variable \(r\) become

\[ S_r(t) = \frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}} \left(1+\frac{\beta_A}{2}\right)P_2\,r - C_2\frac{4Q_iQ_r x}{\omega_0}\,\ddot r - \left(G_2-\frac{P_2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}}\right)\frac{4Q_iQ_r x}{\omega_0}\,\dot r + C_2\beta_\phi \dot r + \left(G_2-\frac{P_2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}}\right)\beta_\phi r. \]

The island-1 thermal forcing becomes

\[ S_{T1}(t) = C_2\frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}\,\dot T_1 + \left(G_2-\frac{P_2\alpha_{A2}}{T_{02}}\right)\frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}\,T_1. \]

For event-response intuition, the homogeneous part of the equation is what determines whether the \(\phi\)-\(T_2\) loop behaves like an overdamped thermal mode or a damped oscillator. The forcing terms determine how KID1 events and \(r\)-quadrature motion enter that oscillator.

Damped-Oscillator Interpretation

The homogeneous characteristic equation is

\[ M_\phi \lambda^2 + \Gamma_\phi \lambda + K_\phi = 0. \]

So the dual-KID feedback branch behaves like a damped oscillator when the second-order reduction is valid. The underdamped condition is

\[ \Gamma_\phi^2 < 4 M_\phi K_\phi. \]

Interpretation of the main parameters:

  • \(C_2\) sets the effective inertia \(M_\phi\).
  • \(G_2\) increases the damping term and also contributes to the restoring term.
  • \(\alpha_{\phi2}\) controls how strongly KID2 temperature motion pulls the phase channel.
  • \(\alpha_{A2}\) enters through the electrothermal correction \(P_2\alpha_{A2}/T_{02}\).
  • \(K_p\) enters only through the restoring term \(K_\phi\), via the factor \(- (2\alpha_{\phi2}/T_{02}) K_p\).

Simplified Form for \(\beta_A=\beta_\phi=\alpha_{A2}=0\) and \(x=0\)

In the operating regime used most often in this project, the beta terms are set to zero, the KID2 amplitude responsivity term \(\alpha_{A2}\) is taken to be zero, and the tone is placed exactly on resonance so \(x=0\). Then the two coupled equations reduce to

\[ \frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\,\dot\phi + \frac{Q_i}{Q_r}\phi - \frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}T_1 - \frac{2\alpha_{\phi 2}}{T_{02}}T_2 = 0 \]

\[ C_2 \dot T_2 + G_2 T_2 + \left(-K_p\right)\phi = 0. \]

After eliminating \(T_2\), the scalar second-order \(\phi\) equation becomes

\[ M_\phi\,\ddot\phi+\Gamma_\phi\,\dot\phi+K_\phi\,\phi=S_\phi(t) \]

with

\[ M_\phi=C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0} \]

\[ \Gamma_\phi = C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r} + G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0} \]

\[ K_\phi = G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r} - \frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p. \]

The deterministic source term simplifies to

\[ S_\phi(t) = \frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}} P_2 r + C_2\frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}\,\dot T_1 + G_2\frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}\,T_1. \]

At exact resonance, the derivative-based \(r\)-coupling terms drop out of the reduced equation, but the direct forcing term proportional to \(P_2 r\) remains because it enters through the island-2 thermal equation. This is still the cleanest form for analyzing whether the feedback branch is overdamped or underdamped. In particular, the feedback-dependent part of the restoring coefficient is

\[ K_{\phi,\mathrm{fb}}=-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p. \]

So in this simplified model the heater feedback changes the effective spring constant but not the effective mass term \(M_\phi\) or the direct damping term \(\Gamma_\phi\).

Sign Analysis in the Simple Case

For the reduced equation

\[ M_\phi\,\ddot\phi+\Gamma_\phi\,\dot\phi+K_\phi\,\phi=S_\phi(t), \]

the homogeneous system behaves like a physical damped oscillator only if the effective mass, damping, and spring coefficients all have the stabilizing signs

\[ M_\phi > 0,\qquad \Gamma_\phi > 0,\qquad K_\phi > 0. \]

In the strict simple limit used above, these coefficients are

\[ M_\phi=C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}, \qquad \Gamma_\phi=C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}, \qquad K_\phi=G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p. \]

Each sign condition can now be read directly in model parameters.

Mass Term

\[ M_\phi=C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}. \]

Since \(\omega_0>0\) and any physical resonator has \(Q_i>0\), the mass term is positive whenever

\[ C_2>0. \]

So the mass sign is automatic for any physical second island with nonzero heat capacity.

Damping Term

\[ \Gamma_\phi=C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}. \]

Because \(Q_i>0\), \(Q_r>0\), \(\omega_0>0\), \(C_2>0\), and \(G_2>0\) for a physical device, both contributions are positive. Therefore

\[ \Gamma_\phi>0 \]

automatically in the simple model. In this limit the loop cannot become anti-damped through \(K_p\), because \(K_p\) does not appear in \(\Gamma_\phi\).

Spring Term

\[ K_\phi=G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p. \]

This is the nontrivial sign condition. A restoring spring requires

\[ G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p>0. \]

Equivalently, if \(\alpha_{\phi2}>0\),

\[ K_p<\frac{G_2 Q_i T_{02}}{2 Q_r \alpha_{\phi2}}. \]

So positive feedback gain reduces the spring constant, while negative feedback gain increases it. In the sign convention of this model, a sufficiently large positive \(K_p\) drives \(K_\phi\) through zero and turns the system from restoring to non-restoring.

If \(\alpha_{\phi2}<0\), the inequality reverses: positive \(K_p\) increases the spring constant and negative \(K_p\) decreases it.

Underdamped Condition

Once \(M_\phi>0\), \(\Gamma_\phi>0\), and \(K_\phi>0\), the system is a stable damped oscillator. It is specifically underdamped when

\[ \Gamma_\phi^2<4M_\phi K_\phi. \]

Substituting the simple-case coefficients gives

\[ \left(C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\right)^2 < 4\left(C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\right) \left(G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p\right). \]

Solving this for \(K_p\) gives

\[ K_p < \frac{T_{02}}{2\alpha_{\phi2}} \left[ G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r} - \frac{\omega_0}{8 C_2 Q_i} \left( C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0} \right)^2 \right] \]

for the common case \(\alpha_{\phi2}>0\). If \(\alpha_{\phi2}<0\), the inequality reverses.

This inequality shows the basic trend clearly:

  • Larger \(C_2\) increases the inertia.
  • Larger \(G_2\) increases both damping and restoring strength.
  • \(K_p\) shifts only the spring term in this limit.

So the first requirement is not underdamping but sign correctness: if \(K_\phi \le 0\), the equation is not a stable damped oscillator at all, regardless of the discriminant.

Decay Time to Equilibrium in the Simple Case

For the homogeneous equation

\[ M_\phi\,\ddot\phi+\Gamma_\phi\,\dot\phi+K_\phi\,\phi=0, \]

the modal decay rates are the roots of

\[ M_\phi \lambda^2+\Gamma_\phi \lambda+K_\phi=0. \]

Therefore

\[ \lambda_\pm = \frac{-\Gamma_\phi \pm \sqrt{\Gamma_\phi^2-4M_\phi K_\phi}}{2M_\phi}. \]

Using the simple-case coefficients,

\[ M_\phi=C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}, \qquad \Gamma_\phi=C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}, \qquad K_\phi=G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p. \]

Substituting gives

\[ \lambda_\pm = \frac{ -\left(C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\right) \pm \sqrt{ \left(C_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}+G_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\right)^2 - 4\left(C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\right) \left(G_2\frac{Q_i}{Q_r}-\frac{2\alpha_{\phi2}}{T_{02}}K_p\right) } }{ 2\left(C_2\frac{2Q_i}{\omega_0}\right) }. \]

Overdamped Case

If \(\Gamma_\phi^2>4M_\phi K_\phi\), both roots are real and negative for a stable system. The return to equilibrium is a sum of two exponentials, and the slow settling time is

\[ \tau_{\rm slow} = -\frac{1}{\lambda_+} = \frac{2M_\phi}{\Gamma_\phi-\sqrt{\Gamma_\phi^2-4M_\phi K_\phi}}, \]

where \(\lambda_+\) is the root with the smaller magnitude real part. The faster transient is

\[ \tau_{\rm fast} = -\frac{1}{\lambda_-} = \frac{2M_\phi}{\Gamma_\phi+\sqrt{\Gamma_\phi^2-4M_\phi K_\phi}}. \]

Critically Damped Case

If \(\Gamma_\phi^2=4M_\phi K_\phi\), the repeated root is

\[ \lambda=-\frac{\Gamma_\phi}{2M_\phi}, \]

so the characteristic decay time is

\[ \tau_{\rm crit}=\frac{2M_\phi}{\Gamma_\phi}. \]

Underdamped Case

If \(\Gamma_\phi^2<4M_\phi K_\phi\), then

\[ \lambda_\pm = -\frac{\Gamma_\phi}{2M_\phi} \pm i\sqrt{\frac{K_\phi}{M_\phi}-\frac{\Gamma_\phi^2}{4M_\phi^2}}. \]

The oscillation frequency is

\[ \omega_d = \sqrt{\frac{K_\phi}{M_\phi}-\frac{\Gamma_\phi^2}{4M_\phi^2}}, \]

and the envelope decays as

\[ e^{-t/\tau_{\rm env}},\qquad \tau_{\rm env}=\frac{2M_\phi}{\Gamma_\phi}. \]

So in the underdamped regime, the settling time back toward equilibrium is controlled by the ratio \(2M_\phi/\Gamma_\phi\), not directly by the oscillation period.

Assumptions

  • The derivation uses the current linearized matrix structure in which \(M(\omega)\) contains only constant and \(i\omega\) terms.
  • This page is the \(K_d=0\) specialization of the combined \(K_p+K_d\) heater-feedback model.
  • The equation is exact for the row-2 and row-4 subsystem of the current model, with \(r\) and \(T_1\) left as explicit source terms.
  • If \(\beta_A=\beta_\phi=0\), all beta-dependent terms above drop out immediately.
