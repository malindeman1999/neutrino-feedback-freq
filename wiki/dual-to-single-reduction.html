Dual-to-Single Matrix Reduction

Start from the single-KID 3x3 model, split into the code's two-KID 4x4 form, then reduce with \(T_2=T_1\) to recover the original 3x3.

Home Theory M Matrix Dual->Single Reduction Power Partitioning

Step 1: Original Single-KID 3x3

Use states \([r,\phi,T]^T\) and write:

\[ M_{3}\,Y_3=N_3, \quad M_3= \begin{bmatrix} m_{11} & m_{12} & m_{13}^{(s)}\\ m_{21} & m_{22} & m_{23}^{(s)}\\ m_{31}^{(s)} & m_{32}^{(s)} & m_{33}^{(s)} \end{bmatrix}. \]

The single thermal coefficients are:

\[ m_{31}^{(s)}=-\left(1+\frac{\beta_A}{2}\right)P, \quad m_{32}^{(s)}=Qx(\beta_A+2)P, \]

\[ m_{33}^{(s)}=i\omega C+G-\frac{P\alpha_A}{T_0}, \quad m_{13}^{(s)}=\frac{2\alpha_A}{T_0}-2Qx\beta_A, \quad m_{23}^{(s)}=-\frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}. \]

Step 2: Split into Two KIDs (Code Form)

Code state is \([r,\phi,T_1,T_2]^T\) with:

\[ M_{4}= \begin{bmatrix} m_{11} & m_{12} & m_{13} & m_{14}\\ m_{21} & m_{22} & m_{23} & m_{24}\\ m_{31} & m_{32} & m_{33} & 0\\ m_{41} & m_{42} & 0 & m_{44} \end{bmatrix}. \]

For the symmetric half/half split used in this project:

\[ P_1=P_2=\frac{P}{2},\; C_1=C_2=\frac{C}{2},\; G_1=G_2=\frac{G}{2},\; \alpha_{A1}=\alpha_{A2}=\alpha_A,\; \alpha_{\phi1}=\alpha_{\phi2}=\alpha_\phi,\; T_{01}=T_{02}=T_0,\; K_{fb}=0. \]

This implies the pairwise equalities:

\[ m_{14}=m_{13},\quad m_{24}=m_{23},\quad m_{41}=m_{31},\quad m_{42}=m_{32},\quad m_{44}=m_{33}. \]

Step 3: Enforce \(T_2=T_1=T\) and Reduce

Start from rows 1-2:

\[ m_{11}r+m_{12}\phi+m_{13}T_1+m_{14}T_2=N_r, \]

\[ m_{21}r+m_{22}\phi+m_{23}T_1+m_{24}T_2=N_\phi. \]

With \(T_1=T_2=T\) and symmetry \(m_{14}=m_{13}\), \(m_{24}=m_{23}\):

\[ m_{11}r+m_{12}\phi+2m_{13}T=N_r, \]

\[ m_{21}r+m_{22}\phi+2m_{23}T=N_\phi. \]

Now add thermal rows 3 and 4 and use \(T_1=T_2=T\):

\[ (m_{31}+m_{41})r+(m_{32}+m_{42})\phi+(m_{33}+m_{44})T=N_{T1}+N_{T2}. \]

With symmetry:

\[ 2m_{31}r+2m_{32}\phi+(m_{33}+m_{44})T=N_T. \]

Evaluate coefficients:

\[ 2m_{31}=-\left(1+\frac{\beta_A}{2}\right)P=m_{31}^{(s)}, \quad 2m_{32}=Qx(\beta_A+2)P=m_{32}^{(s)}, \]

\[ m_{33}+m_{44}= \left(i\omega\frac{C}{2}+\frac{G}{2}-\frac{P}{2}\frac{\alpha_A}{T_0}\right)+ \left(i\omega\frac{C}{2}+\frac{G}{2}-\frac{P}{2}\frac{\alpha_A}{T_0}\right) =i\omega C+G-\frac{P\alpha_A}{T_0}=m_{33}^{(s)}. \]

Also:

\[ 2m_{13}=\frac{2\alpha_A}{T_0}-2Qx\beta_A=m_{13}^{(s)}, \quad 2m_{23}=-\frac{2\alpha_\phi}{T_0}=m_{23}^{(s)}. \]

Step 4: Full Circle Back to the Original 3x3

The reduced equations are exactly:

\[ \begin{bmatrix} m_{11} & m_{12} & m_{13}^{(s)}\\ m_{21} & m_{22} & m_{23}^{(s)}\\ m_{31}^{(s)} & m_{32}^{(s)} & m_{33}^{(s)} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}r\\\phi\\T\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}N_r\\N_\phi\\N_T\end{bmatrix}, \]

which is the same single-KID 3x3 model from Step 1.

So the dual half/half split closes algebraically back to the original single-KID matrix when \(T_2=T_1\) and one thermal equation is removed by reduction.
