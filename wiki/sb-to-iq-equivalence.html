SB to IQ Conversion Equivalence

Verification that sideband-basis to demodulated IQ normalization is identical for single-KID and halfed dual-KID equivalent models.

Home M Matrix Power Partitioning Dual->Single Reduction SB->IQ Equivalence

Goal

Show that the microwave conversion from sideband sources (USB/LSB basis) to demodulated \((I,Q)\) is the same in:

  • single-KID model, and
  • halfed dual-KID equivalent model (equal split with preserved totals).

This isolates whether remaining single-vs-dual differences come from SB->IQ normalization or from later thermal/dynamical coupling.

Shared SB-to-IQ Ingredients

The code uses a shared resonator normalization:

\[ V_{\mathrm{norm}} = I_0 R_0, \] with \(I_0\) and \(R_0\) from the combined resonator parameters.

Python: Sensor.iq_voltage_normalization_V.

It also uses the same passive electrical 2x2 block (no thermal coupling) for IQ transfer:

\[ M_{\mathrm{passive}}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} m_{11}(\omega) & m_{12}(\omega)\\ m_{21}(\omega) & m_{22}(\omega) \end{bmatrix} \] built from \(f_0, Q_r, Q_i, x\).

Python: Sensor.passive_tls_iq_transfer_phi_at_hz().

Numerical Verification (Current Code)

For single vs halfed dual-equivalent construction, the following are equal:

  • \(V_{\mathrm{norm}}=I_0R_0\): both are 3.8398160627287225e-08.
  • Passive Q-transfer magnitude ratio at 1 Hz: 1.0.
  • Passive Q-transfer magnitude ratio at 100 Hz: 1.0.

Therefore the SB->IQ conversion basis is the same in both models.

Conclusion

In the current implementation, SB->IQ normalization is not the remaining source of single-vs-dual mismatch. Any residual difference must come from the full 4x4 thermal/electrothermal dynamics and source coupling after microwave normalization.
